Ignoramus

In the book “Sapiens A Brief History of Humankind” Yuval Noah Harari describes that the scientific revolution happened because human were finally able to admit that ‘we do not know’.

While this probably holds true in broader terms. We still have to try hard not falling into the trap not questioning our self, thinking “I know”. Especially when we worked on something for a while and we are quit proud of it. But maybe someone else did already a better job we just didn’t looked it up (not invented here syndrome)? Or we didn’t try hard enough to reject our hypothesis “The cool thing I build solves our problem”?

If we are not aware of ignoramus we may introduce accidental complexity.
